Club Foundation

On August 4, 1904, the biggest club in the world was born: Independiente

Around 1900, a new store “A la Ciudad de Londres” opened on the southeast corner of Avenida de Mayo and Perú, with entrances on Avenida de Mayo 570 and 600, Victoria 599 and Perú 92. A large European-style store where suits, dresses, shoes, and all men's and women's fashions were available.

The “A la Ciudad de Londres” team was called Maipú Banfield. It participated in friendly matches and inter-commercial challenges with other stores and factories. There was overcrowding in the different lineups, which caused a purge in the various divisions that played friendlies. Obviously, the youngest players were excluded.

A group of young friends who worked as cadets and salesmen felt particularly affected because one of them, Marcelo Degiorgi, was the starting back of the line-up and had to leave without much explanation. This, coupled with the fact that they had to continue paying the same membership fee as the adults who monopolized the starting positions in the football team, meant that, fed up with the situation, they decided to make their own way.

The boys from the household goods, shoemaking and fashion sections began to plan the formation of a new football team so they could play without depending on the decisions of the adults. Finally, on Thursday, August 4, 1904, they decided to hold a meeting of those excluded from the team.

The place chosen for the meeting was the Almacén of the brothers Telésforo and Policarpo Fuertes, which was in front of the store: in Victoria (today Hipólito Yrigoyen) N° 584, in what is currently part of the Palace of the Legislature of the Autonomous City of Buenos Aires. There the eight original members debated the fate of this spin-off. Some suggested joining Atlanta Athletic Club, but in the middle of the debate another of the speakers angrily reproached: “What Atlanta or eight quarters! We have to have an independent club!” And Rosendo Degiorgi shouted excitedly: “There is the name: Independiente! We will call ourselves Independiente.” Needless to say, the motion was approved by acclamation.
